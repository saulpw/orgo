Reactants

  1. Oxygen (O2)

    Molecular oxygen accounts for 21% of Earth's atmosphere. Originally a toxic and corrosive waste product of early bacterial life and archaea, modern aerobic life uses oxygen to dispose of electrons stripped from its food, creating water. (0,0,0,OX)

  2. Hydrogen gas (H2)

    Hydrogen contains more than twice as much energy than gasoline by mass, and burns perfectly in oxygen, producing only water. However, hydrogen gas is virtually nonexistent on Earth, only being created by humans as a byproduct of processing cheaper energy compounds, like natural gas. (0,4,0)

  3. Water (H2O)

    Water is the only pure substance found naturally on Earth in solid, liquid, and gas forms. No other planet in our solar system has the requisite temperature and pressure for all three phases to exist simultaneously. (0,0,0)

  20. urea ((NH2)2CO)

    The human liver neutralizes ammonia, a toxic waste product from the metabolism of nitrogren compounds, into urea, which the body then excretes in sweat and urine. In 1828, Friedrich Woehler accidentally synthesized urea from inanimate chemicals, proving organic compounds did not require a biological "vital force", thus launching the field of organic chemistry. (1,0,0)
